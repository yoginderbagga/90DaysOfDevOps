# Blueprint for the DevOps

#### What is DevOps?

Before understanding DevOps, its important to know what deployment is? Deployment is the process of taking the newply developed system/application, or its update available in the live environment for the people to use. 

DevOps is a software development methodology that combines Development and Operations team to deliver the software faster, efficiently and more securely. DevOps practices enables both the Development and Operations team to speed up the software delivery through automation, collaboration, fast feedback and iterative improvement. 

**HISTORY**  

The DevOps moment was started somewhere in between 2007-2008 when "IT Operations" and "Software Development" teams from those time raised several concern about the gap between both the group one who build the software and one who deploy it i.e the operations teams. Since they both worked as the seperate group they worked in silos, communication gap occured, and slow speed of the software delivery caused major challenges with the deployment. 

**KEY BENEFITS**


> Enhanced Collaboration:  Breaking down the silos between IT Operations and Software Development team which improved the communication.

> Increased Efficiency: By automating the software development lifecycle you reduced the manual tasks, minimize errors, and accelerate delivery times.

> High Quality and Security: With the help of Continous Integration and Continous Delivery, DevOps ensure that software is not only developed faster but also maintain high quality and standards.

> Faster Time to Market: With high collaboration among the teams, DevOps help with speeding up the delivery of the Products from the ideation to the deployment.
